THE FAITH AND LAW OF THE OSMANOĞLU EMPIRE
A Primer for Travellers and Merchants
Published by the Imperial Office of Trade and Foreign Relations, Kostantora
By Appointment to Sultan Velkar

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOREWORD

The Kalovanim Empire welcomes merchants, travellers, and skilled workers from all nations. To ensure your visit is productive and your dealings with the empire's citizens and authorities are smooth, the Imperial Office of Trade has prepared this brief introduction to the customs, faith, and laws of the empire.

Visitors who observe these guidelines will find Kostantora one of the great cities of the world. Those who do not may find their visit concludes at the discretion of the Kazhari corps.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

PART ONE: THE FAITH OF THE OSMANS

The Kalovar people follow the faith brought from the southern desert lands by the Prophet of the Sands some three centuries past. At its core, this faith teaches submission to the one true God, charity toward the poor, justice in all dealings, and brotherhood among believers.

The empire's Sultan is, in temporal matters, the absolute sovereign. In matters of faith, he holds the title of Protector of the Holy Places and Commander of the Faithful — a position that combines political and religious authority in a way that foreign visitors sometimes find confusing.

The faith requires prayer five times daily, at times announced by the calls from the spire-towers of the vethras. Visitors are not required to participate in these prayers but are expected to conduct themselves with appropriate quietude during the call. Loud conversation, commercial transactions, and public entertainment cease during prayer times.

Non-believers are welcome in the empire under the zarqani arrangement: the payment of an additional annual tax grants the right to practice one's own faith privately, own property, conduct business, and live under the empire's law. Proselytizing one's own faith to Muslims is, however, strictly prohibited and carries severe penalties.

The vethras of Kostantora are among the finest buildings in the known world. Non-believers may not enter during prayer times. At other hours, entry with respectful behaviour is generally permitted at the discretion of the vethra's staff.

PART TWO: THE LAW OF THE EMPIRE

The Kalovanim Empire operates under a two-track legal system. The sharia — divine law as interpreted by the empire's religious scholars — governs matters of personal conduct, family law, and religious observance. The kanun — secular law issued by the Sultan — governs commerce, criminal matters, and administrative affairs.

For foreign merchants and travellers, the kanun is most relevant.

Commercial law is enforced by inspectors who operate in the markets and ports. Weights and measures are standardized across the empire. Fraudulent dealing carries severe penalties — a merchant caught using false weights loses his licence, his stock, and a portion of his body that the judge finds instructive.

Criminal law distinguishes between crimes against persons and crimes against property. Crimes against persons are generally punishable by retributive justice — the affected party or their family has the right to compensation or equivalent punishment. Crimes against property are punishable at the discretion of the court.

Slavery is legal throughout the empire. The slave trade is regulated: traders must be licensed, transactions must be registered, and the treatment of slaves is subject to certain legal minimums. Visitors from nations where slavery is not practiced should be aware that the institution is a normal and visible feature of commerce in Kostantoran markets and that expressing vocal opposition to it in public is considered a public disorder offense.

PART THREE: PRACTICAL GUIDANCE FOR VISITORS

Dress: Modest dress is strongly recommended, particularly for those visiting religious sites or formal districts. Women visitors should cover their hair in market areas and vethras.

Commerce: The Grand Bazaar operates from dawn to the evening prayer. Haggling is expected and considered a normal part of commercial exchange. Fixed prices are the exception rather than the rule. The currency of the empire is the gold akche; foreign coins are accepted at most trading establishments at rates that vary — confirm rates before transacting.

Accommodation: The empire maintains a network of caravanserais — resting places for travellers — at regular intervals along the major roads. In Kostantora itself, several establishments cater specifically to foreign visitors.

Security: Kostantora is a well-policed city. The Kazhari patrols are visible at all hours in the main commercial districts. Petty crime exists, as in any large city, but is suppressed more aggressively here than in most. The docks district — particularly after dark — is an exception to this general rule.

The Kazharis: Do not interfere with Kazhari soldiers in the performance of their duties. Do not touch them or their equipment. If stopped and questioned, answer clearly and produce your merchant's letter if you have one. Attempting to bribe a Kazhari is an offense; they are trained and paid specifically to be incorruptible, and the penalties for attempting bribery are considerably worse than whatever they stopped you for in the first place.

PART FOUR: A NOTE ON HISTORY

Some visitors arrive in Kostantora with preconceptions about the city's past — its former name, its former empire, and its former faith. The Imperial Office of Trade respectfully suggests that public discussion of these subjects is inadvisable. The past is past. The Kalovanim Empire looks forward.

Those wishing to understand the history of the city are welcome to consult the approved texts available at the Imperial Library, which offers a comprehensive account of Kostantora's history beginning from the liberation of the city by Sultan Velkar.

Accounts from other perspectives exist, of course. Their circulation, however, is not a matter in which the Imperial Office of Trade is in a position to assist.

We wish you productive trade and a safe journey.
